Engagement Types Our Methodology

Red Teaming vs. Penetration Testing

A Penetration Test is comprehensive but noisy; it aims to find *all* vulnerabilities in a specific system. It asks, "Where are the holes?"

A Red Team Engagement is objective-based and stealthy. It tests your *response capabilities*. It asks, "Can you stop us from stealing the CEO's emails?" We use social engineering, physical access, and custom malware to mimic a real APT (Advanced Persistent Threat).

Why Stress Test Your Defense?

You have bought the firewalls, the EDR, and the SOC service. Red Teaming is the only way to know if they actually work when it counts.

Expose Blind Spots

Defenders think in lists (assets, patches). Attackers think in graphs (relationships). We find the path of least resistance that your scanners miss.

Measure Response Time

It's not about if you get breached, but how fast you detect it. We provide metrics on your "Mean Time to Detect" (MTTD) and "Mean Time to Respond" (MTTR).

Realistic Training

Your Blue Team (defenders) needs sparring partners. A Red Team exercise is the best training day they will ever have, sharpening their skills against human logic.
