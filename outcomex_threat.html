Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Threat Hunting as a Service.

What is the difference between this service and my current security tools?

Our Service is Proactive, Not Reactive. Your current tools (like firewalls and EDR) are designed to stop known threats and generate alerts. Our service uses Threat Intelligence (TI) to generate a hypothesis about unknown threats, and then our human experts Threat Hunt (TH) for those subtle signs that automation misses. We find threats before they alert.

What is "Dwell Time," and how does this service reduce it?

Dwell Time is the critical period an attacker remains hidden in your network before being detected. In a reactive posture, Dwell Time is often measured in weeks or months. Our 24/7 Threat Hunting actively searches for threats, drastically reducing this time to hours or minutes. Rapid Containment procedures are triggered the moment a threat is validated.

Will this service replace the security tools I've already invested in?

No, we enhance them. We perform Seamless Integration with and optimise your existing security stack (EDR, SIEM, Firewalls). Our deep systems integrator expertise ensures your current tools are fine-tuned to receive and act on high-fidelity Threat Intelligence, maximizing your existing investment and ROI.

Why shouldn't we just hire our own internal Threat Hunter?

Cost and Capability. We address the Talent Gap risk. For less than the cost of one single Australian senior security hire, you access a full team of experts, providing 24/7 coverage and diverse, deep knowledge that is expensive and difficult for a single company to acquire and retain internally.

How does Outcomex handle the unique threat environment in Australia?

We provide Local Threat Context. Our Australian-based SOC applies threat intelligence with direct context to local compliance frameworks (like the Essential Eight) and actively tracks regional threat actors, ensuring our hunting efforts are relevant to the threats you actually face.

What happens after a threat is found? Do you just send an alert?

We take Decisive Action. We don't just alert you; our team immediately initiates the necessary steps for Rapid Containment (isolating the host or blocking communication), followed by eradication and restoration planning to dramatically reduce the damage and cost of a breach.

What is the typical onboarding timeline?

The full service launch typically takes 4-6 weeks. This includes three phases: Integration (establishing secure access), Baseline (learning your network's normal behavior), and Go-Live (full 24/7 Threat Hunting begins). We aim for a non-disruptive, rapid deployment.

How does this support my Essential Eight compliance?

It validates that your controls aren't being bypassed. Threat Hunting acts as a critical validation layer, ensuring your mitigation strategies (like Application Control and Patching) are effective against active human adversaries who often find ways around static defenses.
