What is a Security Control Audit?

Organizations often confuse "having a tool" with "being secure." Security Control Auditing validates the effectiveness of specific controls.

For example, you might have Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). An audit asks: "Can it be bypassed via legacy protocols?" You might have backups. An audit asks: "Are they truly immutable against ransomware?" It bridges the gap between policy intent and technical reality.

Deep Technical Verification

Audit Examples

  • Identity Audit Checking for dormant admins, weak MFA policies, and lateral movement paths.
  • Network Segmentation Verifying that IoT devices cannot talk to the Finance server.
  • Email Security Testing DMARC/DKIM/SPF enforcement to stop spoofing.

Why Validate Your Controls?

False confidence is worse than no confidence. Assuming your security works without testing it is the leading cause of silent breaches.

Discover "Shadow IT"

Audits frequently reveal unauthorized software, unpatched servers, and "temporary" firewall rules that were never removed, creating massive backdoors.

Optimize Spend

Why buy a new tool when your existing one is only 40% configured? An audit helps you squeeze maximum value (and security) out of investments you've already made.

Compliance Evidence

Regulators don't trust your word; they trust independent validation. An external audit report is the gold standard for proving due diligence to ASIC or APRA.
